2022 Winter

Uncertain the mountain will catch
Enough flakes and flurries and snow;
The fire to not return,
At least neither to evict
Nor march hooves to the end.
The air has been dense as before,
But it has forced the multitude apart.
Some have resisted the distance.
But obduracy and indignation
Lead gasps, regret, then silence.
Rumor is that war again is coming.
No one cares to first know
That the fetters fathom
By fathom have long since tarried.
The white and cold renewal
Comes around the edge of leaf,
From page of one onto another;
This time may just deliver
The dove that has for very long
Repeatedly been anticipated.
But new as the month may be,
The boisterous laughter is replete
With all things but felicity.
A dozen waxing and waning
May bring them back to life
Or take even more than bargained.
